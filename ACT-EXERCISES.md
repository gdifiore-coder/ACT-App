This document lists all 32 ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy) exercises included in the Mental Fitness Training app, organized by skill category.

## ðŸ§  DEFUSION TRAINING (5 exercises)
*Techniques for unhooking from thoughts*

### 1. "I'm Having the Thought That..." Exercise
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Description:** Add psychological distance from thoughts by reframing them as mental events rather than facts.

**Instructions:**
1. Write down several unhelpful thoughts (e.g., "I'm going to fail," "They think I'm incompetent")
2. Read each thought aloud with weight and gravity
3. Add "I'm having the thought that..." before each statement
4. Read aloud: "I'm having the thought that I'm going to fail"
5. Add another layer: "I'm noticing that I'm having the thought that..."
6. Notice the distance and sense of choice that emerges

---

### 2. Thought Labeling
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Description:** Create distance from thoughts by categorizing them as mental events rather than facts.

**Instructions:**
1. Throughout the day, notice your thoughts as they arise
2. Label each thought type: "judgment," "worry," "planning," "memory"
3. Don't try to change or eliminate them
4. Simply acknowledge: "My mind is having a judgment thought right now"
5. Continue with your work without getting hooked

---

### 3. Passengers on the Bus
**Time:** 7 minutes
**Description:** Visualize thoughts and feelings as passengers you can acknowledge without letting them control your direction.

**Instructions:**
1. Imagine you're driving a bus (your career) with passengers (thoughts, feelings)
2. Notice passengers becoming vocal, threatening, or demanding you change direction
3. Recognize the urge to argue with passengers or pull over
4. Instead, acknowledge their presence
5. Keep driving toward your valued direction while bringing passengers along
6. Notice you can move forward even with uncomfortable passengers present

---

### 4. Just Observe
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Description:** Build the skill of observing thoughts without reacting or trying to change them.

**Instructions:**
1. When negative thoughts arise at work, simply observe them
2. Label them mentally: "That's a thought"
3. Don't analyze where they came from or try to change them
4. Allow thoughts to exist without engaging with their content
5. Practice returning focus to the task at hand

---

### 5. Switching to "Just" Language
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Description:** Minimize emotional reactions by reframing experiences as temporary states.

**Instructions:**
1. Notice when you're experiencing intense emotions at work
2. Switch statements like "I'm terrified" to "I'm just feeling anxious"
3. Use the word "just" to create space: "I'm just worrying," "I'm just stressed"
4. Notice how this creates distance from the experience
5. Continue working while carrying these lighter descriptions

## ðŸ’¥ ACCEPTANCE WORK (5 exercises)
*Building tolerance for discomfort*

### 1. Tug of War with Anxiety
**Time:** 7 minutes
**Description:** Physical metaphor showing how struggling with anxiety makes it stronger, and how dropping the rope creates freedom.

**Instructions:**
1. Imagine playing tug of war with anxiety over a pit at work
2. Notice that pulling harder requires more energy and gets you nowhere
3. Recognize the natural response is to pull, but this keeps you stuck
4. Consider the alternative: dropping the rope entirely
5. Drop the rope and notice you're no longer in a battle
6. Observe that anxiety is still there, but you have freedom to move
7. Notice when you "pick up the rope" to struggle at work

---

### 2. Opening Up to Physical Sensations
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Description:** Practice allowing difficult physical sensations to exist without suppression.

**Instructions:**
1. When physical anxiety hits (racing heart, tight chest), pause
2. Observe and label the sensation without judgment
3. Notice exactly where you feel it in your body
4. Breathe slowly and create space for the sensation
5. Allow it to exist without trying to change or eliminate it
6. Recognize sensations typically peak and pass in about 60 seconds
7. Continue your work task while carrying the sensation

---

### 3. Chinese Finger Trap Principle
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Description:** Learn that pushing into anxiety (acceptance) creates space, while pulling away (avoidance) creates restriction.

**Instructions:**
1. Recall how Chinese finger traps work
2. Notice the automatic urge to escape anxiety
3. Recognize that pulling away makes anxiety tighter
4. Consider pushing into the anxiety instead
5. Move toward anxious situations mindfully rather than away
6. Notice this counterintuitive move creates more freedom

---

### 4. Exploring the Experience
**Time:** 8 minutes
**Description:** Develop curiosity about emotions rather than fear, reducing their power.

**Instructions:**
1. When strong emotion arises, step away briefly if possible
2. Become curious about the experience
3. Observe how the emotion feels in your body
4. Notice thoughts that accompany it
5. Allow yourself to fully experience it without exaggerating
6. Observe the emotion peak and then begin to subside
7. Notice it's less overwhelming than your mind predicted

---

### 5. Giving Emotions Space
**Time:** 6 minutes
**Description:** Allow physical and physiological responses to complete naturally.

**Instructions:**
1. Recognize that emotions create physical responses in your body
2. Give yourself permission for these responses to occur
3. Maintain awareness of emotions during work tasks
4. If you can't process emotions immediately, schedule time later
5. Recall triggering events and allow delayed emotional expression
6. Notice this neutralizes triggers over time

## âš¡ PRESENT MOMENT (5 exercises)
*Grounding and attention skills*

### 1. Five Senses Grounding
**Time:** 3 minutes
**Description:** Rapid grounding technique that brings you into the present moment through sensory awareness.

**Instructions:**
1. Look for 5 things you can see in your workspace
2. Touch 4 different things and notice their texture
3. Listen for 3 different sounds around you
4. Smell 2 different things (coffee, air, etc.)
5. Taste 1 thing (water, gum, or notice taste in your mouth)
6. Return focus to your present task

---

### 2. Tactical Breathing
**Time:** 2 minutes
**Description:** Box breathing pattern for rapid nervous system regulation under pressure.

**Instructions:**
1. Breathe in through nose for 4 counts
2. Hold breath for 4 counts
3. Breathe out through mouth for 4 counts
4. Hold empty for 4 counts
5. Repeat for 4 complete cycles (about 2 minutes)
6. Return to work with clearer focus

---

### 3. Mindfulness of Breath and Body
**Time:** 7 minutes
**Description:** Foundational mindfulness practice using breath and body as present moment anchors.

**Instructions:**
1. Find a moment to pause at your desk
2. Notice your body and the movement of breathing
3. Feel breath entering and leaving your body
4. Notice chest or belly rising and falling
5. When mind drifts to work stress, return to breath
6. Expand awareness to include body sensations
7. Use breath as your anchor to the present

---

### 4. Describe the Moment
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Description:** Build present moment awareness by narrating current experience.

**Instructions:**
1. Describe what you're doing right now: "I am sitting," "I am typing"
2. Include mundane details: "I feel the chair beneath me"
3. Notice neutral or positive aspects of your current experience
4. Avoid judging or analyzing
5. Simply describe observable facts
6. Practice throughout work day

---

### 5. Active Listening
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Description:** Use listening to anchor attention in the present and disconnect from rumination.

**Instructions:**
1. In meetings or conversations, focus entirely on others' words
2. Don't plan your response while they speak
3. Notice their tone, pace, and emotion
4. If alone, listen to ambient sounds: HVAC, keyboards, traffic
5. Let sounds anchor you in the present moment
6. Return to this when mind spirals about performance

## ðŸŽ¯ VALUES CLARIFICATION (5 exercises)
*Identifying what matters*

### 1. Funeral Meditation
**Time:** 12 minutes
**Description:** Powerful exercise connecting you with what you want your career to stand for.

**Instructions:**
1. Get comfortable and close your eyes for a moment
2. Imagine your retirement party or end-of-career reflection
3. Visualize colleagues and friends gathered
4. Listen to what each person says about your contributions
5. In your heart, choose what you most want to hear
6. Let colleagues, mentors, reports speak about your impact
7. Notice what their comments reveal about your values
8. Write down what you heard and wanted to hear

---

### 2. Life Domains Evaluation
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Description:** Comprehensive assessment of how you're living to identify where changes are needed.

**Instructions:**
1. Rate these areas 1-10: Career, Health, Relationships, Skills, Purpose
2. Write reasons for your rating in each area
3. List all strengths in that area
4. List all weaknesses or areas for growth
5. Identify which area needs most attention
6. Notice patterns across domains

---

### 3. Life Book of Possibilities
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Description:** Creates awareness that your career future is unwritten.

**Instructions:**
1. Close your eyes briefly
2. Bring awareness to your breath
3. Open eyes and imagine holding a book called "Your Career"
4. Notice pages written up until now
5. See that tomorrow's page is completely blank
6. Recognize your future is full of possibilities
7. Ask: "What do I want the next chapter to say about me?"
8. Write down what you want to be about professionally

---

### 4. Values-Based Decision Making
**Time:** 8 minutes
**Description:** Four questions to ensure decisions align with core values.

**Instructions:**
1. Identify a work decision you're facing
2. Ask: "Which option best aligns with my core values?"
3. Ask: "What are the specific details of this commitment?"
4. Ask: "Does this honor other people's values?"
5. Ask: "Does this give me direction toward my big picture goals?"
6. Choose the option that best answers all questions

---

### 5. Rediscovering Authentic Values
**Time:** 20 minutes
**Description:** Systematic process to identify core work values.

**Instructions:**
1. List potential work values: integrity, growth, impact, collaboration, excellence, autonomy, etc.
2. Rate each value from 1-10 (10 being most valued)
3. Group related values into themes
4. Identify the word that best describes each group
5. Highlight your top 5 core work values
6. Write rich context for each value explaining why it matters
7. Ensure genuine emotional connection to each

## ðŸ”¨ COMMITTED ACTION (5 exercises)
*Behavior change exercises*

### 1. The Five Why Strategy
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Description:** Identify root motivations behind problematic work patterns.

**Instructions:**
1. Identify a problematic work behavior (e.g., avoiding presentations)
2. Ask yourself "Why do I do this?"
3. Answer honestly
4. Take that answer and ask "Why?" again
5. Continue asking "Why?" up to five times
6. The final answer reveals your core fear or motivation
7. Use this insight to plan different responses

---

### 2. Identifying Valued Actions
**Time:** 12 minutes
**Description:** Connect values to specific behaviors you can commit to.

**Instructions:**
1. Choose one work value (e.g., "competence" or "contribution")
2. Identify current difficulty related to this value
3. Describe internal barriers (thoughts, feelings) preventing action
4. Brainstorm possible actions aligned with this value
5. Choose actions that honor multiple values
6. Commit to taking these specific actions this week

---

### 3. SMART Goals for Performance
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Description:** Create concrete, measurable action plans.

**Instructions:**
1. Write down problematic work behavior needing change
2. Define day-to-day actions to shift the pattern
3. Make goals Specific (exactly what you'll do)
4. Make goals Measurable (how you'll track)
5. Make goals Accurate (truly addresses the issue)
6. Make goals Realistic (achievable with your resources)
7. Set Timeline for check-ins to assess progress
8. Define how you'll handle obstacles

---

### 4. Tracking for Awareness
**Time:** Ongoing
**Description:** Build awareness of behavior patterns through systematic tracking.

**Instructions:**
1. Choose a behavior to track (avoidance, procrastination, people-pleasing)
2. Get a small notebook or phone note
3. Log every instance throughout the day
4. Note time, situation, thoughts, feelings
5. Review daily to identify patterns
6. Use increased awareness to make different choices
7. Continue tracking to maintain accountability

---

### 5. Barrier Identification
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Description:** Map stuck situations to plan forward movement.

**Instructions:**
1. Describe a current work difficulty
2. Identify a value that feels remote due to this situation
3. List internal barriers (thoughts, feelings) between you and the value
4. List your current behavioral responses to these barriers
5. Notice whether responses move toward or away from value
6. Identify acceptance-based alternative responses
7. Commit to trying new response pattern

---

## ðŸŒŒ SELF-AS-CONTEXT (5 exercises)
*Observer perspective work*

### 1. Sky and Weather
**Time:** 8 minutes
**Description:** Establish observer self as container for thoughts and feelings, bigger than any content.

**Instructions:**
1. Think about the sky on a recent day
2. Notice people usually describe the weather, not the sky itself
3. Recognize how changeable weather is
4. Note the sky contains weather but cannot be damaged by it
5. Recognize: "You are the sky. Everything else is the weather"
6. You are the container for thoughts and feelings
7. Notice you are bigger than any work stress or anxiety
8. Difficult content cannot damage your essential self

---

### 2. Observing Self Perspective
**Time:** 7 minutes
**Description:** Develop ability to observe yourself from a detached perspective.

**Instructions:**
1. Recognize you have two perspectives: acting and observing
2. Acting perspective engages with work
3. Observing perspective watches you working
4. Practice shifting to observer view during tasks
5. Watch yourself working without emotional involvement
6. Notice this creates distance and control
7. Use observer perspective during difficult meetings

---

### 3. Interpersonal Perspective Taking
**Time:** 8 minutes
**Description:** Build flexibility by taking others' viewpoints.

**Instructions:**
1. Select a work relationship challenge
2. Close eyes and imagine being the other person
3. View the situation from their perspective
4. Ask: "What were they trying to communicate?"
5. Consider: "What would they do in my position?"
6. Notice what this reveals about your own patterns
7. Practice with different colleagues and situations

---

### 4. Temporal Perspective Taking
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Description:** Shift viewpoint through time to gain wisdom.

**Instructions:**
1. Imagine yourself two weeks ago - what would that version want you to remember?
2. Imagine yourself two years in the future - what advice would you give yourself now?
3. Imagine yourself as a new hire experiencing current stress - observe from experienced perspective
4. Notice what you see in your patterns and responses
5. Use these perspectives to guide current work choices

---

### 5. Retelling Your Professional Story
**Time:** 25 minutes
**Description:** Identify false self-conceptualizations and create authentic narrative.

**Instructions:**
1. Write your current professional self-story: who you believe you are
2. Be honest about harsh self-judgments ("I'm incompetent," etc.)
3. Read through and challenge each point for accuracy
4. Note truth next to false judgments
5. Write objective, non-emotional description of true professional self
6. Commit to embodying authentic identity
7. Notice emotional reactions and recommit when triggered
